📑 Methodology & Glossary

Overview

The KRBL Pricing Simulator quantifies how deliberate changes to a SKU's price and in-store presence are expected to move demand, revenue, and profit. Every scenario is anchored to a historical baseline month of actual performance; the levers you set are then applied and projected through the Analytics team's demand-elasticity model — so commercial teams can pressure-test pricing decisions before taking them to market.

How the Simulation Works

1
Establish the Baseline

The selected month's actual PTC, Store Availability, and Product Ranking are loaded per SKU, alongside the observed sales volume, revenue, and profit.

2
Apply the Levers

You adjust any of three levers — PTC, Store Availability, or Product Ranking — for the editable SKUs. Each change is captured as both an absolute value and a % change from baseline.

3
Project the Impact

A demand-elasticity model converts each lever change into projected volume, then layers in cross-SKU substitution (VTM) to produce simulated volume, revenue, and profit versus baseline.

Simulation Levers & Demand Elasticities

Note: Demand elasticities shown below apply only to IG Everyday 5. Other SKUs remain at baseline and their simulation levers are disabled.

Each lever moves projected volume through an elasticity — the % change in volume for every 1% change in that lever, holding the others constant.

Lever Elasticity Direction Business Effect
PTC (Price to Consumer) −0.605473 Inverse Raising price reduces volume; lowering price lifts it.
Store Availability (Store Count × OSA) +0.165892 Direct Wider distribution and shelf presence increase volume.
Product Ranking −0.283782 Inverse A stronger (lower-number) rank drives higher volume.

Profit basis: unit cost is held constant across the baseline and simulated states at 70% of PTR, so profit responds to movements in volume and price rather than to shifts in cost structure.

Glossary

Baseline
The historical actuals for the selected month, used as the reference point every simulated scenario is measured against.
Simulated Scenario
The projected outcome after your lever changes are applied to the baseline through the demand-elasticity model.
PTC (Price to Consumer)
The retail selling price of the SKU — the primary pricing lever in the simulation.
Store Availability
Store Availability = Store Count × On Shelf Availability (OSA). This represents the effective availability of the product across retail stores.
Product Ranking
The SKU's shelf/search ranking versus competitors, where Rank 1 is best (lower numbers indicate a stronger position).
Elasticity
A regression coefficient measuring the % change in volume for each 1% change in a lever, holding the others constant.
VTM (Volume Transfer Matrix)
Cross-elasticity logic that adds volume gained from — or subtracts volume lost to — substitute SKUs when a simulated change shifts demand between products.
Structural Unit Cost
A fixed per-unit cost held constant across the baseline and simulated scenarios. Structural Unit Cost = 70% × PTR. This fixed unit cost is used throughout the simulation while profit varies with changes in predicted demand and price.
PTR (Price to Retailer)
The selling price at which KRBL supplies products to retailers/distributors before retailer margins.
PTR Calculation
Structural Unit Cost = 70% × PTR.
